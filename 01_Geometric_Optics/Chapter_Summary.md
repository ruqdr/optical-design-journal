# Geometric Optics – Chapter Summary

## Introduction

Geometric optics treats light as rays that travel in straight lines in homogeneous media, bending at interfaces according to Snell's law. This approximation is valid when the wavelength is much smaller than the features of the optical system.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Ray Tracing
- **Ray**: A mathematical line perpendicular to the wavefront, representing the direction of energy propagation.
- **Paraxial Approximation**: Small angles ($\\theta \\approx \\sin\\theta \\approx \\tan\\theta$). Simplifies calculations for first‑order optical properties.

### 2. Refraction and Snell's Law
- **Snell's Law**: $n_1 \\sin\\theta_1 = n_2 \\sin\\theta_2$
- **Critical Angle**: $\\theta_c = \\sin^{-1}\\left(\\frac{n_2}{n_1}\\right)$ for total internal reflection ($n_1 > n_2$).

### 3. Lenses and Lensmaker's Formula
- **Thin‑Lens Formula**: $\\frac{1}{f} = (n-1)\\left(\\frac{1}{R_1} - \\frac{1}{R_2}\\right)$
- **Sign Convention**: Cartesian sign convention (light travels left to right; radii positive if center of curvature is to the right of the surface).
- **Focal Length**: Distance from lens to focal point where parallel rays converge.

### 4. Types of Simple Lenses
- **Plano‑Convex**: One flat, one convex surface. Used for focusing collimated light or collimating a point source.
- **Bi‑Convex**: Both surfaces convex. Common for positive lenses.
- **Plano‑Concave**: One flat, one concave surface. Negative (diverging) lens.
- **Bi‑Concave**: Both surfaces concave. Negative lens.
- **Meniscus**: One convex, one concave surface; can be positive or negative depending on curvature.

### 5. Magnification and Imaging
- **Lateral Magnification**: $m = -\\frac{s_i}{s_o}$, where $s_o$ is object distance, $s_i$ is image distance (measured from the lens).
- **Angular Magnification** (Magnifying Glass): $M = \\frac{25\\,\\text{cm}}{f}$ (for a relaxed eye at the near point of 25 cm).

### 6. Optical Invariants
- **Lagrange Invariant**: $n\\,y\\,\\theta$ is conserved through an optical system (no vignetting).

## Practical Considerations
- **Glass Selection**: Index of refraction ($n_d$) and Abbe number ($V_d$) affect focal length and chromatic behavior.
- **Surface Sag**: For spherical surfaces, sagitta $z = R - \\sqrt{R^2 - r^2}$.
